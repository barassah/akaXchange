About the Aga Khan Academies
The Aga Khan Academies are a network of residential schools established to offer a high standard of education with a focus on leadership, ethics, and pluralism. These academies aim to develop future leaders with a strong sense of social responsibility and global citizenship. Located in Mombasa, Kenya; Hyderabad, India; and Maputo, Mozambique, each academy follows the International Baccalaureate (IB) curriculum and provides students with a holistic educational experience. Below is an overview of each academy:

Aga Khan Academy Mombasa

The Aga Khan Academy Mombasa is located in Mombasa, Kenya. It opened in 2003, and was one of the first Aga Khan Academies. The academy offers education from pre-kindergarten to Grade 12. The curriculum follows the International Baccalaureate (IB) framework, which includes Primary Year Programme (PYP), Middle Year Programme (MYP), and Diploma Programme (DP). The campus spans 7 hectares and includes modern classrooms, science and computer labs, a library, an auditorium, sports facilities, and residential accommodations.

Aga Khan Academy Hyderabad

The Aga Khan Academy Hyderabad is located in Hyderabad, India, and it opened in 2011. The academy offers education from pre-kindergarten to Grade 12. The curriculum follows the International Baccalaureate (IB) framework, which includes Primary Year Programme (PYP), Middle Year Programme (MYP), and Diploma Programme (DP). Situated on a 100-acre campus, the academy features state-of-the-art facilities, including science and computer labs, art studios, music rooms, sports fields, a swimming pool, and residential facilities.

Aga Khan Academy Maputo

The Aga Khan Academy Maputo is located in Maputo, Mozambique. It opened in 2013, and it is the most recent of the three academies which have a residential space. The academy offers education from pre-kindergarten to Grade 12. The curriculum follows the International Baccalaureate (IB) framework, which includes Primary Year Programme (PYP), Middle Year Programme (MYP), and Diploma Programme (DP). The campus covers 18 hectares and includes modern academic buildings, laboratories, libraries, arts and music rooms, sports facilities, and boarding houses.
